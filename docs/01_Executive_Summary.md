## What Is Colibrii Labs?

Colibrii Labs is **Latin America's first real-time AI observatory** — a bilingual (Spanish/English) strategic intelligence platform that monitors, analyzes, and visualizes artificial intelligence readiness, risk, and policy across 20 peer countries, with a primary focus on Costa Rica.

The platform operates as a living analytical tool, combining live data from 4 APIs with 25+ institutional data sources, processed through 10 proprietary algorithms, to deliver actionable intelligence for decision-makers navigating the AI transformation.

---

## The Problem

Costa Rica faces a paradox that defines its AI moment:

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| AI Government Vision Score | **100/100** | Oxford Insights 2024 |
| Actual AI Readiness Score | **0.38/1.0** | Oxford Insights (same index, same year) |
| Binding AI Laws | **0** | Legislative Assembly as of Feb 2026 |
| AI Risk Ranking (WEF 2026) | **#5 globally** (surged from #30) | World Economic Forum Global Risks Report 2026 |
| Jobs at Risk from AI | **40%** | IMF 2024 |
| AI in CR Business Leaders' Top 5 Risks | **Absent** | WEF Executive Opinion Survey 2026 |

The country has world-class vision but near-zero execution. There is no dedicated AI authority, no binding legislation, no national AI investment fund, and no AI certification track at INA (the national vocational training institute that graduates 13,000 IT professionals annually). Meanwhile, the World Bank has classified Costa Rica as an "AI Overperformer" — a country outperforming its income level on AI adoption — creating a narrow window of opportunity before peers overtake it.

## The CAPI-CR Index

The **Colibrii AI Preparedness Index for Costa Rica (CAPI-CR)** is the platform's flagship analytical tool — a 6-dimension composite index that ranks 20 countries on AI readiness using 11 live World Bank indicators plus 2 expert-curated dimensions.

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures | Key Indicators |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| D1 — Digital Infrastructure | 20% | Connectivity and broadband penetration | Internet users (%), Fixed broadband/100 |
| D2 — Human Capital | 20% | Workforce preparedness and education | Tertiary enrollment, Education spending, Unemployment |
| D3 — Innovation | 15% | R&D capacity and tech output | R&D spending, Hi-tech exports, FDI inflows |
| D4 — AI Regulation | 15% | Legal and governance framework | Expert-curated scores (not automated) |
| D5 — Sustainable Energy | 15% | Energy access and sustainability | Electricity access, Renewable %, Consumption/capita |
| D6 — Digital Security | 15% | Cybersecurity posture | Expert-curated scores (not automated) |

The index extends the IMF's AI Preparedness Index methodology with energy sustainability and security dimensions particularly relevant to Costa Rica's profile (99% renewable energy, 2022 Conti ransomware crisis).
