Standards bodies and frameworks for AI governance, safety, and trustworthiness.

- [IEEE 7000 Series — Ethically Aligned Design](https://ethicsinaction.ieee.org/) - Family of standards addressing ethical concerns in autonomous and intelligent systems. `[Free]`

- [OECD AI Principles](https://oecd.ai/en/ai-principles) - Five principles for responsible AI stewardship adopted by 46 countries including several LATAM nations. `[Free]`
- [UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of AI](https://www.unesco.org/en/artificial-intelligence/recommendation-ethics) - First global normative instrument on AI ethics, adopted by 193 member states in November 2021. `[Free]`
- [G7 Hiroshima AI Process Code of Conduct](https://www.mofa.go.jp/ecm/ec/page5e_000076.html) - Voluntary code of conduct for organizations developing advanced AI systems. `[Free]`
- [Bletchley Declaration on AI Safety](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ai-safety-summit-2023-the-bletchley-declaration) - 28-country declaration on frontier AI safety including risks, responsibilities, and international cooperation. `[Free]`
- [Council of Europe Framework Convention on AI](https://www.coe.int/en/web/artificial-intelligence/the-framework-convention-on-artificial-intelligence) - First legally binding international treaty on AI, covering human rights, democracy, and rule of law. `[Free]`
